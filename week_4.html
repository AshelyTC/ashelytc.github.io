Week 4: Design Iteration

This week we went through another iteration of the design we developed last week. Instead of coupling an overhead camera with an eye tracking device, the system would be a tabletop/tablet collaborative application. It would still maintain the eye tracking feature, which will allow the application to gather data on the child’s episodes of visual attention. The collaborative aspect of the system will allow the parent to receive responsive feedback when they have correctly responded to the child’s visual focus (e.g. touching on object that the child is looking at). The type of responsive feedback is to be yet determined, but a couple of ideas could be lighting up LED lights, prompting sound cues, gaining points and leveling etc. By implementing this feature it’ll encourage the parent to adapt visually sensitive communicative behaviors when interacting with their hard-of-hearing or deaf infant.

In addition, we looked at two papers that influenced this new design modification:

  • SPRING: Customizable, Motivation-Driven Technology for Children with Autism or Neurodevelopmental Differences
  • Joint Attention without Gaze Following: Human Infants and Their Parents Coordinate Visual Attention to Objects through Eye-Hand Coordination
