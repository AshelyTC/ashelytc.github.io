Week 1: Prototype Brainstorming

This week we brainstormed potential tech solutions utilizing the knowledge gathered from the following sources:

  • StoryMat
  • Classroom Collaboration in the Design of Tangible Interfaces for Storytelling
  • Nurturing Language and Learning

Our research question is can we improve deaf child – hearing parent interaction and language exposure through enhancing joint-attention for hearing parents. To develop user-centric technology that addresses the obstacles in language learning of deaf children we had to conduct literature review to gain a holistic view of joint-attention development. In this investigation we learned that half way through the first-year, infants (both hearing and deaf) visually focus more on objects than on their parents. Typically, this is where episodes of supported joint-attention take place. These episodes begin with the infant focused on an object (toy truck, pink ball etc) and the parent providing linguistic information related to the infant’s object of interest. This seamless language learning activity occurs if the parent is sensitive and alert to the infant’s attention. However, how do hearing parents facilitate these types of cognitive and language learning opportunities in the case where speech can’t be used to provide linguistic content. If we’re able to encourage the attention supporting strategies used by deaf parents of deaf children through our assistive technology, we will provide hearing parents an aid in nurturing their deaf child’s language development without relying on speech.

The first functionality of our tech solution is to detect the infant’s and parent’s visual focus. Because head position is an accurate proxy for gaze attention we decided that having an overhead camera coupled with a mat can be used to track the infant’s head positions scene by scene. We’ll investigate state-of-the-art head tracking libraries to see the current tools at our disposal. In addition, we’ll present our research proposal to Ally and Dr. Hall to obtain constructive feedback to further guide the project progress.
