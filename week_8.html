Week 8: System Design Specifications

This week I worked on formalizing the design specs of the system. We’re basing this on our design goals, which are the following:

  • Age-appropriate activity for child (6-18 months)
  • Non-intrusive face-to-face communication between parent and child

So, with these goals in mind here’s a brief rundown on what specific technologies we are planning on using:

  • Name: Eye-tracker
    • Description: Collects data on child’s eye focus and delivers it to the rest of the system.
    • Goal: Make the system aware of where the child is looking at and at what specific time interval. This information will be delivered to the wearable or AR device, prompting the appropriate signals for the parent.
  • Name: Visual/Haptic Wearable
    • Description: Wearable LED/haptic device for the parent, where green light indicates child’s visual focus on object and red when child’s attention is off-task (i.e. at researcher, system, parent etc.). The device will deliver a haptic response if parent initiates successful moments of joint-attention.
    • Goal: The wearable device is light-weight and only visible to the parent, providing a non-intrusive mode of visual communication. This won’t distract the child and give the parent the freedom to be both attentive to the interaction and child’s attention focus. In addition, both visual and haptic signals are delivered to the parent to encourage reinforcement of successful communicative behaviors.
  • Name: Portable AR device
    • Description: Augments the corresponding ASL translation of each object on table for parent’s reference.
    • Goal: Help parent provide linguistically rich interactions without the obstacle of ASL non-fluency. In addition, it will encourage linguistic input to be delivered in the most accessible modality for the deaf child during successful moments of parent initiated joint-attention.
