Course Description

Language exposure from caregivers is critical for neuropsychological and language development for young children from birth. Deaf children with hearing parents, unfortunately, are deprived from this essential language environment. The goal of this project is to explore design space of intelligent user interfaces that augment parent-child communication through enhanced social interaction (e.g. adaptive joint attention) and accessible American Sign Language that cope with everyday needs. The study will involve literature review (e.g. ASL acquisition, parent-child interaction, and assistive technologies), research question/hypothesis development, contextual inquiry, system prototyping (e.g. eye tracking, augmented and wearable user interface) and preliminary evaluation. The student will obtain research and technical skills to carry out rigorous and independent research. We will also focus on academic planning, writing and presentation skills, and the student is encouraged to share research outcomes as research paper/poster to HCI and assistive technology venues (e.g. ASSETS), and other student academic symposium.
